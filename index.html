Project Overview

Brief Overview

Objectives: This project explores the ethical implications of AI-driven healthcare, focusing on the DeepMind-NHS data-sharing controversy and the balance between innovation and patient rights. It examines key ethical concerns such as privacy, autonomy, and consent through frameworks like utilitarianism, deontology, and virtue ethics while assessing legal considerations, including GDPR compliance and AI ethics guidelines. Ultimately, the project aims to provide recommendations for ethical AI development in healthcare to ensure transparency, accountability, and patient trust.

Timeline: The project will begin with research and data collection on the DeepMind-NHS case and relevant ethical theories in Weeks 1-2, followed by an analysis of legal and professional implications, including GDPR and AI ethics guidelines, in Week 3. In Week 4, solutions and recommendations for ethical AI governance will be developed, leading to the compilation of findings and finalization of the project report or poster in Week 5.

Resources: The project will utilize case studies on the DeepMind-NHS controversy and similar AI healthcare cases, along with ethical frameworks from Thomas H. Givinsâ€™ Ethical Worksheet. Legal documents, including GDPR and UK Data Protection laws, will be analyzed alongside AI ethics guidelines from organizations like IEEE and WHO. Additionally, academic papers and expert analyses on AI ethics in healthcare will provide valuable insights.
